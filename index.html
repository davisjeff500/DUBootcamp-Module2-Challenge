About Me

'DamWatch' was a COTS product developed by US Engineering Inc, and was purchased by USDA in a 25-million-dollar contract in the Fall of 2013 to provide crucial real time data monitoring of the nation’s 14,000 dams and dam related structures. The DamWatch project was deemed a ‘Redball’ Mission Critical application by President Obama in 2014, and was given highest priority by the USDA to bring it online. Damwatch was foreseen by President Obama and the USDA to eventually provide government employees and civilians real time data of to the country’s public and private dams’ current physical and environmental conditions, and be accessed through an easily understandable Web UI.

In retrospect, the DamWatch project constantly generated new hurdles, both technical and personal, and I believe the project highlighted my ability to take on new intellectual challenges, deal with pressures of mission critical software development, and expanded my interpersonal skills required to work with politicians, engineers, civil servants, and end users, while facing everchanging Project business and technical requirements. In an age of separation of duties within the Federal Government, I was able to be a major part in all aspect of Damwatch’s SDLC and gave me the abilities I bring to a workplace.
